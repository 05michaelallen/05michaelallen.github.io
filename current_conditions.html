My name is Michael Allen. I am a PhD student in the Department of Geography at UCSB, working in remote sensing of urban surface climates, particularly surface temperatures. In 2017 I completed a MSc in Physical Geography at the University of Western Ontario. My thesis develops a method to derive 3-dimensional urban surface temperature and provides the first time-contious, geometrically representative analysis of the surface urban heat island effect.

My research interests include:

- Developing new methodology for measuring and modelling urban surface temperature.
 - Radiative transfer in urban/complex environments.
 - Critical analysis of the rapidly expanding and evolving literature on urban surface climates.
