My name is Michael Allen. I am a PhD student in the Department of Geography at UCSB, working under the advisement of Dr. Joseph P. McFadden. I work primarily in remote sensing of urban climates, with particular focus on the importance of the surface in determining human thermal comfort, energy usage, and vegetation health at a variety of scales. In 2017 I completed a MSc in Physical Geography at the University of Western Ontario. My thesis, written under the advisement of Dr. James A. Voogt, develops a method to derive 3-dimensional urban surface temperature and provides the first time-continuous, geometrically representative analysis of the surface urban heat island effect.

My research interests include:

- Radiative transfer in urban/complex environments.
 - Development and assessment of new methodology for measuring and modelling urban surface temperature.
 - Estimation of the influence of urban thermal effective anisotropy on the accuracy of remote sensing of urban thermal climates.
 - Critical analysis of the rapidly expanding and evolving literature on the effect of urban development on the climate.
